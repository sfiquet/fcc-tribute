Marie Curie

Pioneer of radioactivity research

1903 Nobel Prize portrait

In a Nutshell

  • First woman to be awarded a Nobel Prize.
  • Only person to win a Nobel Prize in two different sciences (physics and chemistry)
  • Discovered two radioactive elements, radium and polonium
  • During WWI, developed and set up radiography units to identify the location of shrapnel and bullets in injured soldiers.
  • Died aged 66 from leukemia caused by exposure to radiation
  • Her papers from the 1890s are so radioactive that they are kept in lead-lined boxes and those wanting to consult them need to wear protective clothing
  • Her remains and her husband's were transferred to the Pantheon in 1995 in honour of their achievements

Timeline

  • 1867Born Maria Salomea Skłodowska in Warsaw on November 7
  • 1878Death of her mother. Marie is 10 years old.
  • 1883Graduates from school with a gold medal. Regular universities being closed to women in the Russian-controlled Kingdom of Poland, she then studies at the clandestine Flying University.
  • 1891Moves to France to study at the University of Paris
  • 1893Obtains a degree in physics
  • 1894Obtains a second degree in mathematics
  • 1895Marries Pierre Curie in Sceaux
  • 1897Birth of her first daughter Irène
  • 1903Obtains her PhD in physics
  • 1903Nobel Prize in Physics, shared with her husband Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their research on radiation
  • 1904Birth of her second daughter Ève
  • 1906Pierre dies in a road accident. Marie is offered his chair at the department of Physics of the University of Paris. She is the first woman to become a professor at the university.
  • 1911Nobel Prize in Chemistry, for the discovery of radium and polonium and the isolation and study of radium
  • 1914-1918Develops radiology services for wounded soldiers, including a fleet of mobile radiology units, 200 field hospital radiology units and 150 radiologists that she trains herself. The X-ray units are used to treat an estimated one million soldiers.
  • 1918-1934Head of the Radium Institute in Paris (now Curie Institute)
  • 1934Dies in Passy, Haute-Savoie on July 4
